"The predicted plant species list provided herein was generated using the BIEN Conservation Assessment Suite (v1.0.0), which queries BIEN species distribution model (SDM) range maps and occurrence records via the BIEN R package (Maitner et al. 2018, Methods in Ecology and Evolution 9:373–379).

This list represents modeled predicted presence based on correlative species distribution models (MaxEnt) trained on Americas-wide herbarium and plot records. No species presence has been confirmed by field survey within the study area. Individual species may be incorrectly included (false positives) or missing (false negatives).

Known limitations: (1) Stacking binary SDM predictions systematically overestimates local species richness (Calabrese et al. 2014); the total species count is an upper bound. (2) SDM model quality metrics (AUC, Boyce Index, training record count) are not available per-species via the BIEN API. (3) Western Amazonia is severely undersampled in BIEN — most records come from navigable river corridors. (4) Climate predictors at 2.5 arc-minute resolution cannot distinguish várzea, igapó, and terra firme forest communities. (5) Native/introduced status is incomplete for Amazonian taxa. (6) Confidence tiers (High/Moderate/Low/Very Low) are heuristic thresholds, not statistically calibrated probability classes.

This output is suitable for preliminary screening and field survey prioritization only. It must not serve as the sole or primary basis for conservation assessments, environmental impact statements, protected area designations, or any legal determination. Field verification by qualified botanists is required before operational or regulatory use."

⚠ Important Warnings — Please Read Before Use

  1. Modeled Predictions Only — Not Field-Verified Records. All species are predicted based on SDMs. No field survey was conducted.
  2. Uneven and Incomplete Data Coverage. Remote tropical regions (western Amazonia, Guiana Shield, Andes) are substantially undersampled. A short list may reflect data poverty, not species poverty.
  3. Stacked SDM Overestimation. Stacking binary SDM predictions overestimates local richness (Calabrese et al. 2014, Global Ecology and Biogeography 23:1365–1372). The species count is an upper bound.
  4. Confidence Tiers Are Heuristic. High / Moderate / Low / Very Low tiers reflect data support patterns, not calibrated probability-of-presence classes. "High" does not mean "confirmed present." % AOI covered (fraction of the assessment area predicted suitable) drives tier assignment; % Range in AOI is shown as a supplementary endemism indicator only.
  5. Native vs. Introduced Status Is Incomplete. Native status is derived from BIEN occurrence metadata, which is partially absent for Amazonian taxa. Species flagged "likely_introduced" should be reviewed; species flagged "status_unavailable" cannot be classified from available data.
  6. Taxonomy Follows BIEN Scrubbed Names. Names may differ from Plants of the World Online, Flora Neotropica, or other authorities. Check synonymy before citing.

About This Tool

Columns in the species table:

  • N Occ (polygon): BIEN occurrence records clipped to the drawn polygon and deduplicated by rounded coordinates. This is the count used for confidence tiers.
  • % AOI covered: Fraction of the assessment area (AOI) covered by the species' modeled range polygon. Used in tier assignment. Higher = stronger spatial support.
  • % Range in AOI: Fraction of the species' total Americas-wide range that falls inside the AOI. An endemism indicator — high values suggest narrow-range species.
  • Native Status: Majority-vote from BIEN native_status field. "likely_native" / "likely_introduced" / "status_unavailable."
